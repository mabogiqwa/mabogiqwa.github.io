Inside the Black Box: How Neural Networks Learn

It's been a desire of the ages to enable computers to think. These desires date back at least to times of Ancient Greece. Figures such as Galatea, Talos, and Pandora in Greek tales may be regarded as artificial life.

When programmable computers were first created, people had already wondered whether we could create machines capable of conscious thought. This was a hundred years before Ada Lovelace, 1842 built the first one. Nowadays, computational intelligence/AI is a thriving field which is almost ubiquitous in most of the technology we interact with. We look to intelligent software to automate routine labour, understand speech, analyse images, make diagnosis in medicine and support basic scientific research.
