Scalars, Vectors, Matrices, and Tensors

A scalar can be viewed as a real number. Scalars are written in italics. We also denote them in lowercase variable style forms. For example, we might say that let \(s \in \mathbb{R} \) be the slope of a line while defining a real-valued scalar. A scalar can also be seen as a matrix with a single entry which would imply that \(a = a^T \).

A vector is an array of numbers. Numbers that arranged in order. Vectors are denoted in a lowercase bold typeface. The first element of a vector x is \(x_1 \), the second element is \( x_2 \) and so on. You could say that the first element of a vector is denoted by it's name in italic typeface with a subscript. Example of a vector is:

x\(\hspace{0.5em} = \begin{bmatrix} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ . \\ . \\ x_n \end{bmatrix} \)

We also have that \(x_{-1} \) denotes a vector that contains all elements of x excerpt \( x_{1} \). Let \(S = \{1, 3, 6 \} \) then \( x_{-S} \) is the vector that denotes all the elements of \( x \) excerpt 1, 3, and 6.

if \(\hspace{0.3em}\) x \(\hspace{0.4em} = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 2 \\ 3 \\ 4 \\ 5 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} \hspace{0.3em} \) then \( \hspace{0.4em} x_{-S} \hspace{0.3em}\) = \(\hspace{0.3em} \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \\ 5 \end{bmatrix} \)
